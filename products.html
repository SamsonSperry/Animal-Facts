A Mile Below The Surface Of Water Anglerfish

Anlgerfish have one of the strangest ways to reproduce. The male Anglerfish will bite onto a female and will never let go, eventually the female will grow around the male and will absorb him into her body, allowing her to have children at anytime she would like.

Flat, Rotund, & Evolutionary Masterpieces Crabs

There is a specific term for when a decapod crustacean evolves into a crab, this is known as Carcinization. This most likely occured due to similar environmental pressures across the world. The most interesting thing about these crustacean decapods (like lobsters and shrimp) is that even though they appear as a crab, they are not technically a crab. This is because they are not a part of the same taxenomic group as standard crabs.

Tropical & American Caecilians

Caecilians are considered to be legless amphibians, making them similar to salamanders and frogs. They live in Central and South America. There are more than 200 different species and they range from 3.5 inches to 5 feet long.
